Diary Study App

Period: 2020

Expertise: dev, React Native

Team: personal project

Making Diary Study More Efficient

This project was for a mobile app development course. The main part of this project is developing a working mobile application, hence the design portion of it is not complete due to the limited time. In the following I will share how I come up with the project, how I design (roughly) and develop the app, and what is the next step of the project.

Introduction

Motivation

While conducting a diary study for another project of mine, Talkie, I realized that there’s not too many tools available for researchers and participants to use. In our case, we created a Google spreadsheet for participants to log their observations, and asked them to upload photos to a separate Google drive folder.

Problems

It is inconvenient to use tools like Google sheet for several reasons. First, it’s easy for participants to forget to log a diary during the day. Second, the logging behavior has a greater chance of happening on mobile, e.g. participants notice something worth logging, they will use their phone to take a picture, and probably add some note to it. This app is built to make the activity of diary study more efficient and effortless.

Competitive analysis

Before starting building the app, I conducted a brief competitive analysis. Since there’s not too many competitors in the market, I looked at some indirect competitors such as Indeemo and FocusVision. Key findings from the analysis:

1.

Participants can share the diary in a social-media-like settings, which is most users are used to

2.

Researcher can communicate with the participants in real time

Guerilla Interview

Besides competitive analysis, I also talked with a few researchers about their experience of conducting diary study. The findings are as follows:

1.

It’d be critical for the app to remind participants to log a diary, and have a intuitive interface that allows users to quickly enter key information when an event happens

2.

It’d be helpful if the products provides an interface for researchers to customize the diary template and analyze the results after the study

Key Features

Participants side

Researcher side

Based on the research results, the app should have two parts – participants side and researcher side. The participants side is for participants to accept a project from a researcher. The researcher side is a dashboard where researchers can create a (diary study) project and invite participants to join. In addition, the logged data from participants should also be shown in the dashboard so researchers can analyze them easily.

Wireframe

I created a wireframe to follow while developing. For the sake of the project scope and time limitation, I only design the wireframe for the participant side, and only include the “Diary Entry” and “Calendar View” features.

Development (Demo)

The development focuses on the participants side. The demo here shows how participants can log a diary in the app. Technology used for building this app includes React Native + Expo (front-end), and Firebase / Firestore (back-end). While trying to utilize existing iOS modules as much as possible for the matter of efficiency, there’s no module for certain elements such as the “calendar entry interface” which I built by myself.

Future Work

I’m currently working on the design of the rest of the product, and planning to build and roll out the whole product in the near future. If you’re interested and would like to work on this project with me, hit me up and let’s work together!
